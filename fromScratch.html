  • Snowboarding has its roots in the 1960s, when Sherman Poppen invented the “Snurfer” as a toy that combined surfing and skiing
  • Modern snowboarding developed in the 1970s and 1980s, with pioneers like Jake Burton Carpenter and Tom Sims creating specialized boards and equipment
  • The sport grew rapidly in popularity during the 1980s and 1990s, especially in the Mountain West as ski resorts began allowing snowboarders
  • The first major milestones for snowboarding in the Mountain West were
    1. Brighton Resort, Utah becoming one of the first ski areas to allow snowboarding (early 1980s)
    2. Breckenridge, Colorado hosting early snowboard competitions (1985)
    3. Snowboarding debuting in the Winter Olympics in Nagano, Japan (1998), boosting its popularity across resorts in the region
